The Problem

Textile Waste Crisis

Massive volumes of clothing and fabric waste are discarded into landfills every year in the UAE.

Emergency Supply Gaps

Basic first-aid and emergency materials remain expensive or unavailable during crises and disruptions.

Single-Use Dependency

Medical textile products are disposable, costly, and environmentally unsustainable.

Circular System Engine

1. Collection Network

Textile waste is collected from homes, schools, factories, and community drives.

2. Sterilization & Sorting

Materials are cleaned, sterilized, and classified based on fiber strength and usability.

3. Fabric Engineering

Waste textiles are transformed into standardized emergency fabric sheets.

4. Kit Manufacturing

Sheets are folded into slings, wound covers, masks, padding, and thermal wraps.

5. Distribution

Kits are supplied to families, schools, and clinics at low cost.

6. Circular Re-entry

Used materials can re-enter the system, continuing the circular loop.

Our Solution

RAHA transforms discarded textile waste into sterilized, reusable emergency fabric systems. Instead of single-use bandages, it produces standardized fabric sheets that can be adapted into multiple emergency functions. This reduces environmental waste while ensuring accessible emergency preparedness tools for all communities.

Why Fabric Sheets?

Traditional emergency materials are single-use, expensive, and dependent on fragile supply chains. RAHA replaces them with reusable, multi-functional fabric sheets that can be folded into slings, masks, wound covers, and thermal wraps — reducing cost, waste, and dependency during crises.
